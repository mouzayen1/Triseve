Le Rituel

One crème. Three intentions.

Every ingredient in N° 333 serves one of three purposes. Nothing decorative. Nothing to fill the label.

Hydrater

The water phase draws moisture in and refuses to let it leave. Distilled water carries humectants that bind water to skin for hours — not the illusion of hydration, the fact of it.

Distilled water · Vegetable glycerin · Sodium hyaluronate · Provitamin B5

Nourrir

The oil phase feeds skin the lipids it recognises as its own — jojoba that mirrors sebum, avocado that sinks deep, squalane that returns what time takes away.

Coconut oil · Avocado oil · Jojoba oil · Squalane · Coco-caprylate

Protéger

The veil seals the work — an olive-derived emulsion that behaves like skin's own barrier, guarded by vitamin E and a living ferment instead of synthetic preservatives.

Olivem 1000 · Cetyl alcohol · Vitamin E · Radish root ferment

La Formule Intégrale

Thirteen ingredients. Nothing to hide.

Most body lotions are water thickened with petrochemicals and scented to cover it. N° 333 reads like a recipe — because it is one. Open each line. We will tell you what it is, and why it earned its place.

13

What it isWater, steam-distilled until nothing remains but water itself — free of chlorine, heavy metals and dissolved minerals.

Why it belongsEvery cream begins with water; ours begins with the purest form of it. Distillation removes the impurities of treated tap water that can unsettle delicate botanicals — so each ingredient that follows performs exactly as intended.

What it isA humectant derived from plant oils — a molecule that attracts water and holds it against the skin like a magnet.

Why it belongsConventional lotions often lean on propylene glycol, a petroleum derivative that can irritate sensitive skin. Plant glycerin does the same work more gently — drawing moisture into the surface of the skin and keeping it there, hour after hour.

What it isCetearyl olivate & sorbitan olivate — an emulsifier crafted entirely from olive oil, the first of our three sèves. It binds water and oil into one silken emulsion.

Why it belongsIts structure mimics the lipids of your own skin barrier, so the cream absorbs like something skin recognises rather than sits on top of. Mass-market creams use PEG emulsifiers — ethoxylated petrochemicals. Olivem is the natural-origin alternative the finest green formulators reach for.

What it isA fatty alcohol derived from coconut — nothing like the drying alcohol in hand gels. It is a soft, wax-like emollient that gives the crème its dense, cushioned body.

Why it belongsThis is the "good" alcohol: it conditions and smooths instead of dehydrating. Where cheaper lotions borrow their slip from silicones that merely coat the skin, cetyl alcohol builds a texture skin can actually drink in.

What it isThe whole oil of the coconut, rich in lauric and caprylic fatty acids — lipids closely related to those found naturally in skin.

Why it belongsIt softens the skin that works hardest — knuckles, elbows, heels — and leaves a supple, conditioned finish. Conventional creams reach for mineral oil, a petroleum by-product that coats without nourishing. Coconut oil feeds the skin while it protects it.

What it isOil pressed from the flesh of the avocado — the second sève — dense with oleic acid, phytosterols, and vitamins A and E.

Why it belongsMost oils rest near the surface; avocado is prized for how deeply it absorbs, carrying its vitamins where drier skin needs them and supporting the skin's own moisture barrier. It is the reason N° 333 keeps working long after it has vanished into the skin.

What it isThe third sève — technically not an oil at all but a liquid wax ester, structurally near-identical to the sebum your skin produces on its own.

Why it belongsBecause skin reads jojoba as its own, it absorbs without a trace of grease and helps balance skin that is either too dry or too oily. Conventional lotions imitate this weightless finish with dimethicone; jojoba achieves it the way nature drafted it.

What it isA feather-light, plant-derived emollient — the stable twin of squalene, the moisturising molecule your skin makes naturally but produces less of with every year.

Why it belongsSqualane returns what time takes away: instant suppleness with zero after-feel, and it will not clog pores. Synthetic substitutes — silicones, mineral oil — only film the surface. Squalane replenishes it.

What it isA light ester made from coconut fatty acids — the ingredient responsible for the crème's fast-absorbing, dry-silk finish.

Why it belongsThis is nature's answer to silicones: the elegant, powdery slip of dimethicone without the plastic film it leaves behind. Entirely coconut-derived and readily biodegradable — luxury that rinses clean from the world.

What it isThe stable, water-soluble form of hyaluronic acid — the famous hydrator that can hold up to a thousand times its weight in water.

Why it belongsIt plumps the fine lines of hard-working hands and keeps skin visibly dewy hours after the crème has absorbed. Ordinary body lotions rarely include it — it is costly, and they are built to a price. We consider it essential.

What it isA provitamin that your skin converts into vitamin B5 on contact — one of the best-documented soothing and conditioning agents in skincare.

Why it belongsPanthenol calms visible redness, softens rough patches and supports the skin's own recovery — exactly what hands ask for after washing, weather and work. Where conventional lotions quiet stressed skin by filming over it, panthenol works with the skin instead. Gentle enough for the most reactive complexions.

What it isTocopherol — a natural antioxidant that shields skin lipids from oxidative stress and keeps our botanical oils as fresh as the day they were blended.

Why it belongsConventional formulas stay shelf-stable with synthetic stabilisers such as BHT — another petrochemical on the label. Vitamin E does the same guardian work while actively conditioning the skin it touches. One ingredient, two gifts.

What it isA preservative born of fermentation — radish root ferment, from the same lactic-culture tradition that has kept food safe for centuries.

Why it belongsEvery honest cream must be preserved; the question is how. Instead of parabens, phenoxyethanol or formaldehyde-releasers, N° 333 is kept safe by a ferment accepted by natural-cosmetics standards. Protection, without the compromise.
